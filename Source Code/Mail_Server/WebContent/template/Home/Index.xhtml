About CRS University

CRS prioritizes outreach to Catholic colleges, Catholic universities and Newman Centers because college students are today’s most socially-minded, compassionate, eager, and effective change agents. Catholic campuses across the country play a critical role in informing and steering the national dialogue on today’s most pressing justice issues.

College and university staff can partner with CRS to help equip their student leaders with resources and programs for campus education and activism. Faculty can rely on CRS to provide quality resources to assist their research and supplement their syllabus in areas of global concern.

CRS is using innovative approaches to help the world’s poorest people lead better lives. Explore the CRS 2013 Interactive Annual Report to learn more.

Mission and History of CRS

View this 20-minute presentation given by Chris Tucker, Director of Special Projects at CRS, to understand the transformations in CRS’ evolving view of its mission and identity: from reconstruction of Europe to global scope (1950s); from resource-transfer to economic development (1970s/80s); from humanitarian focus to “justice lens” on right relationships (1990s).

How CRS Views Partnership

This 15-minute presentation by Sarah Ford, Senior Technical Advisor, Partnership, Capacity Strengthening, describes how partnership is a consistent feature of CRS work, guided by solidarity and subsidiarity. Imbalance of resources can be challenging: CRS may be grant-holder but its work is local. Capacity-strengthening is therefore critical at all levels of CRS’ work, particularly in its work with U.S. colleges and universities.
